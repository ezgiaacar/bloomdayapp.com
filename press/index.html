About

The short version

BloomDay is a mobile task planner that rewards you for getting things done — not by gamifying stress, but by growing something beautiful. Every completed task waters your garden. Plants sprout, bloom, and fill your personal landscape over time.

Built entirely by one person with no coding background, BloomDay started as a personal project during a career transition and became a fully functional app with 131 hand-designed plant species, three garden themes (cozy interior, English garden, and allotment), focus mode, streaks, time capsule letters, and a home screen widget.

The app is built with React Native (Expo), Supabase for authentication and sync, RevenueCat for subscriptions, and Resend for transactional emails. Every plant, every scene, and every pixel was designed by the founder.

Founder

The story behind BloomDay

EA

Ezgi Acar

Founder, Designer & Developer

Ezgi spent eight years in humanitarian protection work with organizations including UNHCR, IOM, and various NGOs focused on refugee response. She holds an M.S. in Gender and Women's Studies from METU and a B.A. in Philosophy from Bilkent University.

After a period of unemployment, she taught herself to code and built BloomDay from scratch — designing every plant, writing every line of code, and building the website and marketing entirely on her own. No CS degree, no coding bootcamp, no team.

Her humanitarian background — figuring out what people need, working with limited resources, staying composed when things break — turned out to be surprisingly transferable to product development.

Long (full)

BloomDay is a mobile task planner that turns everyday productivity into a living garden. Instead of streaks that shame you or points that feel hollow, BloomDay rewards every completed task by growing a real plant in your virtual landscape. Over time, your garden fills with the results of your daily effort — a visual, personal record of consistency.

The app features 131 individually hand-designed plant species across three garden themes: a cozy interior room, an English garden, and a countryside allotment. Additional features include a built-in focus mode timer with ambient sound, daily streaks, weekly growth reports, shareable garden snapshots, time capsule letters to your future self, and a home screen widget.

BloomDay was built entirely by Ezgi Acar, a former humanitarian protection worker with eight years of experience at UNHCR, IOM, and refugee-focused NGOs. With no coding background, she taught herself React Native and designed every plant, every screen, and every pixel from scratch. The project started during a period of unemployment and career transition — proof that the skills learned from working in crisis environments transfer remarkably well to building products.

BloomDay is available on iOS and Android. The app is free to download with an optional premium subscription.
